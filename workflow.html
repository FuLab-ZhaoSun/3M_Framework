Integrated Management of Biobank

Biobank Management

Biobank management encompasses the entire lifecycle of samples, with its core processes including standardized accession and release management mechanisms.

Sample Accession Process

During the accession process, the applicant (clinical department/project leader) should first submit the "Biological Sample Accession Application Form" along with supporting materials such as the research plan. After the biobank comprehensively evaluates the project's scientific value, ethical compliance, and feasibility, it enters the approval process. For approved projects, the applicant should further submit a standardized collection plan (including collection, transport, and processing details) and sign an "Accession Agreement" with the biobank, clearly defining the execution of informed consent and the procedure for requisitioning consumables. In the final stage, both parties jointly verify the sample information and complete the "Accession Registration Form," digitally archiving it according to the encoding rule "hospital code + organ code + serial number + sample type + tube segment number." Samples that fail the review will be issued a "Notice of Accession Rejection," stating the specific reasons.

Sample Release Process

Regarding the release process, the applicant should submit the "Release Application Form" with departmental and ethical approvals 14 working days in advance. After the biobank reviews the research significance and compliance, projects that pass the review need to sign a "Release Agreement" to define rights and responsibilities. Then, staff locate and verify the target samples, confirm inventory information collaboratively with the applicant, and simultaneously complete the signing of the "Release Registration Form" and the physical handover. To ensure the effectiveness of sample usage tracking, the applicant needs to submit sample application progress reports 1/3/6 months after sample release, forming a closed-loop management system to continuously optimize the allocation of resources within the bank.

Metadata Management

Metadata management integrates patient clinical data (collected from case systems and follow-up records) and histological data through a unified identifier system, ensuring the uniqueness of the association between the biobank and the database.

Data Accession Process

In the data accession process, the data-requesting department first submits the "Biobank Data Accession Application Form" (data associated with already accessioned samples may be exempt from application, but an application is required for cases where informed consent was signed but no sample was actually donated). Subsequently, biobank management personnel conduct a comprehensive assessment of the project's research value, data appropriateness, and accessibility. If the data fails the review, a "Data Accession Rejection Notice" is issued with reasons. Data that passes the review will be entered into the database strictly according to specifications. Upon completion, the data is organized, archived, and the "Biobank Data Accession Registration Form" is filled out. The entire process involves scrutiny for data legality and ethical compliance.

Data Release Process

Data release management requires the requesting department to submit the "Biobank Data Release Application Form" with ethical approval. During the review process, it undergoes dual verification by both the data source department and the biobank (including assessment of data-sample matching, research value, and feasibility). If the data fails the review, a "Data Release Rejection Notice" is issued. After approval, staff will organize and archive the data according to requirements. Finally, designated personnel from the applicant obtain the data via on-site copying or encrypted transmission, while completing the "Data Release Registration Form" for handover.
